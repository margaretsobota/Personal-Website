About Me

My name is Margaret Sobota and I am a computer science student at Northwestern University. I love both web and game development, and I am always trying to find projects to work on.

Last summer, I was a remote intern at a company called ACUHO-I where I worked on a web application for their annual conference. This application serves to make navigating the conference easier for attendees and has taught me a lot about the client-driven approach to coding. Additionally, I built this website last summer to showcase my work and experience.

Since then, I have honed my skills and am currently working on a number of exciting projects. Currently, I am working in a team of six people to develop a mobile application built in React Native to allow Northwestern students to efficiently match themselves with prospective student mentors on campus. Also, I am working with a team to build another web application built in React that acts as a personalized journaling tool for therapy patients. Finally, I am working on an Agile team to develop a top-down, puzzle game using Unity and C#.

Though computer science is my passion, I have other hobbies as well. I love hiking and specifically backpacking. Also, I am a portraiture artist and I love to experiment with different painting techniques. If interested in my art, my website can be found here .
